Conclusion Questions:

  1. Reflect on the creative process you used. What was useful? Discuss your reflection with your partner and then write a reflection individually.

        I knew starting out that I would rather make a game than a story as I am better at coding more complex game functions than making interesting storyline, so I had that determined from the start. My partner also seemed more interested in the game idea than the story, so we just started thinking about games that we would like to play if they existed. One thing we focused on was making modifications to real-life games that we liked. For our final idea, initially called “Two Player Bullet Hell”, I was inspired by the real life game “Realm of the Mad God”, which is a player vs AI online game that requires players to dodge AI bullets and kill the AI with their own. Our informal modification on this idea was to make the game two-player, rather than against an AI. We did change other elements of the game as well and simplify the concept a lot to make it fit into Scratch, and our time limit for creation, but the main inspiration was the “bullet hell” part anyway, which was preserved. Most of our other game ideas had some basis in games that already existed to some extent, but with some sort of twist to make them unique to us.

  1. Reflect on the team dynamic. What helped the team work well together? Discuss your reflection with your partner and then write a reflection individually.

        The team was able to work well together because of team members’ ability to specialize and integrate specialization effectively. As I am good at coding, I took charge in writing most of the code for our game, developing the logic and algorithms. My partner, however, is more of an artist, and he helped by choosing the design for sprites, coordinating colors, and creating all the backgrounds that required modification. Additionally, my partner commented the code to ensure that he would be comprehensive of what I had written. Thus our specialization allowed us to more effectively complete the project.

Design Brief

Client:

A local construction company

Designers:

Aviram Bhalla-Levine, Christopher Garduno, Lemar Popal, Sarang Vadali

Problem Statement:

Some very heavy materials must be lifted on a nearby construction site. Local workers need a way to lift these materials because they can not do so easily by hand.

Design Statement:

Design, build and test a prototype mechanism that can lift 100 g up 30 cm to aid in the creation of an actual lifting mechanism for use in construction.

Constraints:

  • Must lift 100 g 30 cm
  • Must be operable by a switch
  • Must use only materials in class
  • Must be completed by Tuesday the 28th of October
  • Must be presented on Friday the 31st of October

Deliverables:

  • A working demonstrable prototype
  • A working NetLogo code for the prototype
  • A neat electronic report that includes:
  • Title page
  • Table of contents
  • Design brief
  • NetLogo screenshots and code
  • 4 annotated final design photos
  • Descriptions and math explanations for the final design
  • A reference list if necessary
  • Individual Deliverables:
  • Calculations and values chart
  • Conclusion questions

Calculations

  1. Determine the work done by the system.
    1 kilogram ~ 9.80665 Newtons
    Force = 400 g = 3.922 N
    1 centimeter = 0.01 meters
    Distance = 30 cm = 0.3 m

N * m = Joules

3.922 N * 0.3 m = 1.18 Joules

  1. Determine the output power of the system.
    Joules (j) = 1.18 joules
    Seconds (sec) = 44.29 seconds

Joules/seconds = watts

1.18 Joules / 44.29 seconds = 0.027 watts

  1. Determine the input power of the electrical system.
    7.5 mA = 0.0075 A
    Current (I) = 0.0075 A
    Volts (V) = 8.84 V

Power = Current * Voltage


0.0075 A * 8.84 V = 0.0663 watts

  1. Calculate the efficiency of the system.
    Output = 0.023 watts
    Input = 0.0663 watts

Efficiency = output/input


0.027 watts / 0.0663 watts = 0.347 = 40% efficiency

Final Design Solution

       


                                                                                                

The purpose of this creation, as stated before is to move a 100g weight up 30cm to prototype a construction winch. This mechanism converts electrical energy to linear kinetic energy with a winch. We first used an energy source of 8.84v and 7.5 mA or .067 watts volts to power a motor which was connected to a gearbox. The gearbox was meshed to a gear on the same axle as the drum. This configuration allowed the winch to pull 3.9922 newtons .3 meters in 44.29 seconds. 3.9922 newtons for .3 meters is 1.18 joules, divided by the time resulted in .027 watts. We then divided the initial energy, .067 watts by the output energy of .027 watts to derive an efficiency of 40%.
